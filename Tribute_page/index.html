Getulio Vargas

the father of a nation.

Among Getulio's achievements as Brazil's leader, here are a few to note.

  • 1909 Elected to the Legislative Assembly of Rio Grande do Sul.
  • 1911 Vargas married fifteen-year-old Darci Lima Sarmanho in March.
  • 1926 Vargas went on to serve as Minister of Finance under newly-elected President Washington Luís.
  • 1930 Wins the national election, becomes president of Brazil.
  • 1937 'Estado novo' regime implemented in response to possible communist coup.
  • 1945 Vargas resigns.
  • 1951 Gets elected as president once more.
  • 1954 Commits suicide.Shot himself in the chest with a pistol.

Vargas's Legacy

When learning about Brazil's history there are many important names to note, from emperors, to former slaves. However, there is one, that arguably, stands out from the rest, his name, is Getulio Vargas.

While many democratic achievements happened during his rule, like the right for women to participate in the national elections, the right to secret vote (ending the common practice of the elite manipulating the people's vote) and the CLT, a declaration of worker's rights, during the Estado Novo regime, Vargas was responsible for antisemitic, anticomunnist and ultranationalist legislations. Therefore, seizing the people's basic right to freedom of expression and thought.
